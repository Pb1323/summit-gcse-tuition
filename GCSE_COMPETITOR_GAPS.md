# GCSE Niche — Competitor Research & Gaps

Last updated: 2026-07-11.

## Landscape
- **Revision content** (Seneca Learning, Cognito, BBC Bitesize, Save My Exams, MasteryMind) is crowded and mostly free or cheap — don't compete here, you'll lose on content volume and brand recognition alone.
- **1:1/group tutoring marketplaces** (GoStudent, Tutorful, Explore Learning, First Class Learning) have severe, *documented* trust problems on Trustpilot:
  - Inconsistent tutors — families assigned multiple different tutors in short succession, no continuity.
  - Hard-to-cancel subscriptions with surprise auto-renewal charges (£200+/mo until cancelled).
  - Sessions starting late, ending abruptly, kids left "hands raised" waiting for help in groups.
  - Ignored emails, poor customer service, disputes biased toward parents over tutors.

## The gap
No major player combines **exam-condition mock simulation + transparent billing + consistent marking** without the multi-tutor churn and subscription traps above. This is the same trust-first positioning Summit Tuition (11+) already uses — directly reusable here almost verbatim: no auto-renewal traps, one clear point of contact, manual marking, results only released once actually marked (not vague "attend and hope" content consumption).

## Recommendation
Don't build another revision-content library — build the **mock exam + honest feedback loop** lane instead, and lead marketing with the specific trust failures above (a parent who's been burned by a £200/mo surprise auto-renewal is a warm lead for "transparent, cancel-anytime, no tutor-roulette").
